# 🌧️ Telangana State Weather Data Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes rainfall data across districts and mandals in Telangana State using **Power BI** and **Power Query**. The goal is to uncover seasonal trends, regional variations, and actionable insights that support decision-making in agriculture, infrastructure planning, and climate preparedness.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Visualize rainfall data across districts and mandals.  
- Identify seasonal and regional rainfall patterns.  
- Provide actionable insights for agriculture, water storage, and disaster preparedness.  

---

## ❓ Problem Statement
- Rainfall data is scattered and hard to interpret across districts.  
- Seasonal patterns are unclear, affecting planning and agriculture.  
- Stakeholders lack a visual tool to explore and compare rainfall trends.  

---

## 🔍 Key Observations
- **Warangal Urban & Rural** districts receive the highest average rainfall.  
- Rainfall peaks in **July and August**, indicating strong monsoon concentration.  
- **Yearly Decline**: Average rainfall dropped from 0.08 mm in 2021 to 0.04 mm in 2024.  
- **Daily Extremes**: Day 27 recorded peak rainfall of 113K mm, while Day 20 dropped to 5K mm.  
- **District Comparison**: Mulugu consistently receives more rainfall than Hyderabad.  

---

## 📊 Insights
- **Mulugu’s higher rainfall** makes it a strong candidate for water storage infrastructure and flood preparedness.  
- **Declining annual rainfall** signals potential long-term climate shifts impacting agriculture and water availability.  
- **District-level variation** highlights zones for water harvesting and irrigation support.  

---

## 🖥️ Tools & Technologies
- **Power BI** – Interactive dashboards & visualizations  
- **Power Query** – Data cleaning & transformation  
- **Excel** – Pre-processing and validation  

---

## 📈 Business Impact
- Supports **crop planning** by identifying critical rainfall periods.  
- Helps in **reservoir placement** and **flood management**.  
- Provides **localized water management strategies** for districts.  

---
## 🏞️ Dam Recommendations
Based on rainfall analysis:
- Mulugu → Large dam for flood control & irrigation
- Warangal → Medium dam for seasonal storage
- Bhadradri Kothagudem, Nirmal → Check dams for local irrigation
- Kamareddy, Sangareddy → Micro-dams for water harvesting

## ✅ Conclusion
The analysis reveals significant seasonal and regional rainfall variations across Telangana. With rainfall declining year-on-year, this project emphasizes the need for **climate preparedness, irrigation planning, and water harvesting strategies**.
